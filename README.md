# Project Overview
The Digital Literacy Project was designed in a role-based scenario where the student takes on the role of a Student Digital Ambassador. The goal of this project is to help students effectively navigate the digital world by raising awareness about key digital literacy skills, online etiquette, and professional digital presence.

This project focuses on equipping students with practical knowledge such as:

- Creating and maintaining professional online profiles
- Understanding safe and responsible internet usage
- Using digital tools for communication, collaboration, and data handling
- Gaining basic exposure to coding and online assessment platforms

This project is designed to comprise several tasks, which involve theoretical as well as practical application, finally resulting in the creation of the Digital Literacy Portfolio. Moreover, the project report was prepared in detail to reflect the entire process of each task, showing the theoretical as well as practical application.

# 🧩 Tasks Overview

The project consisted of five major tasks:

- Task 1: Creation of a digital infographic covering digital literacy, professional presence, and online safety  
- Task 2: Development and updating of professional online profiles  
- Task 3: Google Form creation with response tracking and a beginner coding challenge  
- Task 4: Writing formal emails and creating a social media responsibility checklist  
- Task 5: Cyberbullying case study and online safety checklist  

Each task was documented in a detailed project report.

# 📂 Module Summaries

Module 1: Introduction to Digital Literacy & Presentation Tools

This module introduced the concept of digital literacy, emphasizing its importance in academic and professional environments. It covered fundamental skills required to operate effectively in the digital space.

It also included hands-on exposure to presentation and content creation tools such as:

- Canva, Prezi, and Miro
- Cooltext for text design
- Understanding and using GIFs

The module focused on enabling students to visually communicate ideas through digital platforms.

Module 2: Building a Digital Portfolio

This module focused on the importance of creating a professional digital identity. Students learned why maintaining an online portfolio is essential for career growth and networking.

Profiles were created or explored on platforms such as:

- LinkedIn and GitHub (core professional platforms)
- Quora and Stack Overflow (knowledge-sharing platforms)
- Kaggle, ResearchGate, TechGig (skill-based platforms)
- TED-Ed and Wiki Education (learning platforms)

The module emphasized credibility, visibility, and professional branding in the digital world.

Module 3: Digital Tools & Coding Platforms

This module introduced commonly used collaborative and productivity tools, along with beginner exposure to coding platforms.

Topics included:

- Google Workspace tools (Docs, Slides, Sheets, Forms)
- Creating and managing online forms and responses
- Overview of coding platforms such as HackerRank, HackerEarth, and CodeChef
- Introduction to collaborative learning environments

The goal was to build familiarity with tools used in academic and professional workflows.

Module 4: Email & Social Media Etiquette

This module focused on professional communication skills, especially in formal digital environments.

Key areas covered:

- Structure and format of formal emails
- Guidelines for writing professional emails
- Workplace communication etiquette
- Responsible use of social media

Students practiced drafting emails for real-world scenarios, reinforcing clarity, tone, and correctness.

Module 5: Cybersecurity & Online Safety Awareness

This module addressed critical aspects of cybersecurity and digital safety. It highlighted different types of cybercrimes and preventive measures.

Topics included:

- Cybercrimes (financial, personal, and social)
- Common fraud methods and phishing attacks
- Cybercrime flow and reporting mechanisms
- Preventive practices for safe online behavior

Special emphasis was given to:

Secure online transactions (including UPI safety)
Awareness of reporting channels such as the National Cyber Crime Portal and helpline service 1930
